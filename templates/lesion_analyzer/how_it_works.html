Best Practices for Images
  • Good Lighting: Ensure the image is well-lit with natural or bright white light
  • Sharp Focus: Make sure the lesion is in clear focus without blur
  • Appropriate Distance: Capture the lesion from an optimal distance to show detail
  • Proper Framing: Include some surrounding healthy skin for context
  • High Resolution: Use high-quality images (minimum 224x224 pixels)
Limitations & Considerations
  • Not a Replacement: AI analysis cannot replace professional medical diagnosis
  • Visual Analysis Only: Based solely on visual features, not clinical history
  • Confidence Scores: Lower confidence scores may indicate uncertain cases
  • Image Quality: Poor quality images may lead to inaccurate results
  • Early Stage: Very early or subtle lesions may be challenging to detect
Model Validation & Accuracy
